This I made from pictures of wooden blocks and their shadows. At some point I decided to create glyphs out of the shapes following some simple design rules. Then I drew those in chalk, and photographed them. Then I made a basic grammar system with those glyphs, purely to describe a single construction I made where the blocks form a square shadow.

Later on I made a book out of just the glyphs you see here, even the ones that never got used. It was exhibited at Single Surface on a table.

My friend Joe printed them for me on some old off-white paper I found somewhere. They are joined with wet-activated mounting/hinging tape. I do not recommend trying to bind books in this manner.

Though I read and thought about some stuff when making it, I don't really have any specific deeper meaning to give you about this work.
