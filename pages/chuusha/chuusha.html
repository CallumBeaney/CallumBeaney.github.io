英訳：織田作之助「注射」
A Translation of Oda Sakunosuke's “Injection”

This is a short story penned by modernist writer Oda Sakunosuke (1913 - 1947), who was famous for characterising Japan's post-war identity breakdown in short stories about the listlessness of modern life as part of the 'Buraiha' (無頼派) or "decadent school" literary movement.

In recent years, artist-photographers have begun incorporating such literature into their photobooks e.g. Oda's story Horse Racing (競馬) appears in Moriyama Daido's Odasaku (織田作). This story was taken from Yokota Daisuke's book Heartpressed as designed by Machiguchi Satoshi (shown adjacent).

I have tried to maintain Oda’s relatively unadorned style while preserving the somewhat old-fashioned language, and the vocabulary used to the greatest extent. To that end, this is a ‘hard’ translation that prioritises the information in each sentence over any literary merit the translation might otherwise develop. The paragraph breaks match the book’s layout exactly.




注射をするくらいなら死んだほうがマシだ、病気をしても注射までして助かろうと思わぬと言っていた｡　——　それほど注射の嫌いな男であった。

"Rather than take an injection, dying would be preferable. Even in sickness, I would never think to go as far as to take a shot" – His hatred of injections ran to such an extent.

注射の針の中には、悪魔の毒気がはいっていると信じ込んでいる頑冥な婆さん以上に、注射というものを恐れていた。伝染病がはやって、町内一人残らず予防注射を受けるべしというお達しがあったとき、まるで夜逃げ同様に他の土地へ旅行した。が、その土地でもまた伝染病がはやっていて、汽車が着くなり取っ摑って、予防注射をさえることになったが、自分の順番が来て、看護婦に腕を消毒されながら、医者の持っている針を見た途端、卒倒してしまったという。それほどの注射ぎらいの尾崎が急に注射マニアになってしまったのだから驚く。実は細君が病気になってからだということである。

He was terrified of syringes, even moreso than his obstinate grandmother, who firmly believed that the needle's hollow contained a demonic evil. With an epidemic spreading rapidly, the order that not a single person in the neighbourhood may remain unvaccinated spurred him to travel to a different region, as if he were a convict fleeing the law in the dead of night. The contagion, however, had spread to that place too, and Ozaki was caught and prepped for immunisation immediately upon arrival. His turn in line came. A nurse disinfected his arm, but the moment that he saw the needle that the doctor was holding, he fainted. That Ozaki would quickly develop such an enthusiasm for injections is astounding. As a matter of fact, it was the result of his wife's illness.

細君の病気は胃癌であった。癌の苦痛というものは、これはもう地獄の苦しみである。この世の中で、こんな痛さがあるのかと、世を呪い神を呪いたくなるくらいの激痛である。生きながらの地獄だ。

It was stomach cancer. The pain was already hellish. If one were to ask of such agony, it could only be said to be a pain intense enough to make one wish to spite the god who cursed the world with it – a living hell.

看護婦は一時間置きに、モルヒネが少しはいっている鎮痛剤のロンパンを注射した。はじめの内、尾崎は注射の針を見るのが辛いので、細君が注射を訴えた途端に、隣の部屋へ逃げ出していたが、細君の激痛が看護婦が一人でアンプルを切ったり、アルコールで消毒したりしている時間を待てなくなって来ると、尾崎も細君の苦痛を一秒でも早く和らげてやりたさに、看護婦の注射をこわごわ手伝うようになった。

The nurse would administer a morphine-based painkiller called Ronpan [N.B. This is heroin] at one hour intervals. At first, Ozaki found the sight of the needle too much to bear, and upon hearing his wife resort to another dose he would flee to a nearby room, however seeing her pain intensifying, and seeing the nurse working alone and cutting the ampule without time to sterilise it, in wanting to save even a second of pain for his wife, Ozaki fearfully came to assist the nurse with her injections.

しまいには、夜中看護婦が眠っている間、細君の激痛がはじまると、尾崎が看護婦に代って、見よう見真似で注射してやるようになった。だんだんそうしている内に尾崎の注射は看護婦より巧くなった。

In the end, Ozaki would work on behalf of the night nurse when she slept whenever his wife's pain began, having learned to do so by imitation. Gradually, he came to work more skilfully than the nurses.

間もなく細君は死んだ。

Suddenly, his wife died.

苦しみながら死んで行った細君は尾崎の恋女房であった。
ありし日の細君の面影に悩まされて、眠れぬ夜が続いた。
睡眠剤を服んでみても効かなかった。

She died, Ozaki's dear wife, in agony.
Tormented by her face in memories of bygone days, sleepless nights became endless.
He would try taking a sleeping aid, but to no effect.

赤い眼をこすりながら、尾崎はふと細君用に買って置いたロンパンの残りを想いだした。鎮痛剤のそのロンパンを注射すると、細君は今まで転げまわるように苦しんでいたのがまるで嘘のように、静かに深い眠りに落ちて行ったのである。

Rubbing red eyes, Ozaki suddenly remembered that there was still some Ronpan leftover. When he injected the sedative, his wife - who had until now writhed in pain - would fall into a deep sleep as though it were never there.

尾崎は不眠の辛さにたまりかねて [堪り兼ねて]、背に腹は代えられぬロンパンをこわごわ自分の腕に注射してみた。すると、ぐっすり眠れた。

Unable to bear this bitter restlessness, and knowing that he had to make some sacrifice to make some gain, Ozaki fearfully tried injecting the Ronpan into his arm, thereupon sleeping soundly.

その日から注射が怖くなくなった。脚気の気味のある尾崎は、それまでオリザニン　vit b brand　の粉末を服んでいたが、粉末より注射の方が効くとわかると、ヴィタミンB1の注射薬を買い込んで、毎日自分の腕に注射した。会社で同僚にも注射してやると、喜ばれた。

Following that day, injections no longer scared Ozaki. Feeling a loss of sensation in his feet, Ozaki took some Orizanin Vitamin B powder, but upon realising that injecting it would be more successful, bought up a lot of Vitamin B1 ampoules, and shot a dose of it into his arm, every day. Ozaki was delighted to give injections to his colleagues at work.

他人の腕に針を差し込む瞬間のスリルがふと快感めいて来た。カルシウムを注射してくれたと頼む若いタイピストのむっちりした柔い腕へ、プスリ [とは、針が皮膚に入り込む音を表示する言葉] と針を差し込む時など、胸の血がドキドキと血管の中で鳴っていた。

Inserting a syringe's needle into other people's arms came to feel unexpectedly pleasant, even thrilling. He was asked by a young typist to shoot a dose of calcium into her plump, soft arm. The moment when he pushed the needle into her skin made the blood in his chest thump and the blood in his veins sing.

尾崎はヴィタミン、カルシウム、葡萄糖、ズルファミンド、ホルモン、鎮痛鎮静剤、睡眠剤、解熱剤、その他注射薬と名のつくものなら何でも買い溜めた。肩の凝りに効く注射薬も尾崎の薬箱にはいっていた。月給の大半を注射宅に注ぎ込み、貯金まで引き出した。
注射にいるアルコールはどんな高い闇値でも買い込んだ。
そしてそれを無料で注射してやることが、唯一の薬しみになってしまった。罹災した時も注射器と注射針だけは持ち出した。無一文になったが、それだけが唯一の財産だった。

Ozaki stocked up on vitamins, calcium, glucose, antibiotics, hormones, pain relief sedatives, sleeping aids, fever medicine, anything that had "dose" in the name. He also put some drugs to help his sore shoulder into his medicine box. The majority of his monthly salary was poured into such medicines, before drawing on his savings.
He bought up alcohol for his ampoules however high the black market price.
And finally, administering injections for free became his only source of pleasure. When downtrodden and afflicted, his injector and hypodermic needle were all he took with him. He became penniless, those items his only property.

ある日社用で旅行して宿屋に泊まると、美しい女中が薄団を敷きに来て、尾崎が肩の凝りの注射をしているのを見ると、自分にもしてくれという。尾崎は素知らぬ顔でロンパンを注射してやった。女中は急に欠伸をして、「私眠くなったわ。ああいい気持！　ここで一寸横にならせて下さいね」

One day, while out on a trip for his company, a beautiful inn maid came to lay the futon in the room in which he stayed, and, upon seeing Ozaki inject some muscle relaxant for his stiff shoulder, asked him to give her a dose too. Without betraying his intentions through his expression, he instead injected her with Ronpan. She quickly yawned, "I've become sleepy, it feels nice! Could I lay down here for a little bit?"

そして瞬く間に眠ってしまった。二時間後、眼を覚した女中は、眠っている間尾崎が自分にどんなことをしたか、すぐ悟ってしまったが、怒りもせず夢心地のうっとりした眼で尾崎を見た。

In an instant, she fell asleep. Awakening two hours later, she wondered what Ozaki had done to her, and though quickly realising his crime, she looked at him absentmindedly and without anger.

旅行から帰ると、日ごろ虫の好かない課長が尾崎を呼んで、近ごろ夜眠れぬので困っているんだが何か良い注射はないかといった。

After returning from his holiday, Ozaki's boss, whom he found disagreeable, called him, wondering whether there were any good drugs that could help his recent sleepless nights.

尾崎はヒロポンという興奮剤を二CCひそかにへ注射を吸い上げた。ヒロポンは一CCの半分注射しただけでも、一晩中徹夜で仕事が出来るという覚醒剤である。

Ozaki secretively syphoned off 2 cc of a stimulant called Philopon [N.B. This is methamphetamine]. Even injecting half a cc of it would keep a person up for an entire night without hampering their work the next day.

課長はそれと知らずに、ああこれで今夜は眠れると喜んで帰って行ったが翌朝鰯のような赤い眼をしばたたきながら出社すると「尾崎君、君に注射して貰ったが、かえって眼が冴えるばかしで、夜中に煙草ばっかし吸うていたよ。闇の煙草を四十円も吸ったよ」
「そうですか。効きませんでしたか。余程頑固な不眠症ですね。じゃ、こんどはべつの睡眠剤を注射しましょう」

Unaware, Ozaki's boss left to return home, pleased to know he would sleep well that night, however the following morning, he came in to work with red eyes like a sardine's. "Ozaki, you gave me an injection, however I'm all the more awake, and spend the whole night doing nothing but smoking black-market cigarettes. I had 40 of them".

"Oh really? It wasn't effective? That's quite a stubborn sleeping problem. Well then, Let's try injecting a different sleeping drug."

尾崎は何食わぬ顔で、またヒロポンを取り出して、素早くレッテルをはがすと、今度は三CC吸い上げた。
「随分沢山入れるんだね」
「いやこれぐらい入れないと効きませんよ」

With an innocent look, Ozaki took out another Philopon dose, and quickly tore off the label, this time syphoning off 3 cc.
"You're putting an awful lot in!"
"Ah, but if we don't use this much, it'll not take effect".

翌朝、出社した課長は瀕死の病人のように青ざめながら、カンカンに怒っていた。

The next day, Ozaki's boss, with a pale face as though a patient on the verge of death, came into work in a rage.

尾崎は馘首になった。退職金はすでに注射宅の購入のため前借りしていたので、その日から食うに困ってしまった。

Ozaki was dismissed. Having already spent his severance pay, and already used his final month's advance, his days would become troubled for hunger.

尾崎は虎の子の注射薬を風呂敷の上にひろげて、毎日闇市の片隅に佇んだ。しかしそれも
売りつくしまうと、尾崎はまた無一文になってしまった。

Ozaki spread tiger cub tranquilliser over a wrapping cloth, loitering in a corner of the black market every day. But even after running out of stock, Ozaki would still be penniless.

やがて尾崎は浮浪者の仲間入りをした。そして何日かたったある日、川に浮いている尾崎の死骸が発見された。

Eventually Ozaki joined a group of tramps. And after a number of days, his corpse would be discovered floating in the river.

検視の医者は不思議そうに首をひそねった。実は尾崎が入水する直前、懐にたった一つ残っていた防腐剤を自分で注射して置いたということを、医者は知る由もなかったのである。

The physician conducting the autopsy tilted his head in bewilderment. In his breast pocket was a sole vial of preservative chemical, the significance of which he couldn't understand. In actuality, Ozaki had injected himself with it immediately, before diving headfirst into the river.

その防腐剤の注射薬だけはさすがに闇市でも売れなかった。
そしてそれが尾崎の最後の注射になってしまったのだ。

That preservative was not sold even on the black market.
And so, that turned out to be Ozaki's last injection.
